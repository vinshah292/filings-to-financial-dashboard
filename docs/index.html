01

Revenue Bridge

  • Total advisory revenue grew $348M over four years, from $2,270M to $2,618M (+15%). All three segments contributed.
  • Corporate Finance added $152M, Financial Restructuring $136M, and FVA $60M.
  • The bridge is a net view. The path was not a straight line: revenue fell to $1,809M in FY2023 when M&A froze, then recovered to a record in FY2026.
02

Revenue Mix

  • Corporate Finance is the engine and the swing factor: $1,593M → $1,107M in the downturn → $1,745M five-year high in FY2026. It drives the shape of the total.
  • Financial Restructuring is the stabilizer, peaking at $544M as credit stress rose. FVA is the steady base, low-volatility fee events.
  • FY2026 mix: Corporate Finance 67%, Financial Restructuring 20%, FVA 13%.
03

Counter-Cyclical Hedge

Restructuring rises as Corporate Finance falls

What the data says

  • Indexed to FY2022=100, the two books move opposite: Corporate Finance fell to 69 by FY2024 while Financial Restructuring climbed to 133. That is the hedge.
  • The lift is lagged. In the FY2023 trough, Corporate Finance dropped 29% but total revenue fell only 20%, because Restructuring and FVA held flat, a diversification cushion. Restructuring's own counter-cyclical surge landed the next year, in FY2024.
  • Restructuring then peaked in FY2025 as the M&A recovery took hold, and eased as Corporate Finance took back over.
